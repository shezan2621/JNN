Chapter 2

When Friendship Became Something More

2017–2020
♡

If the first chapter was about how our story began, this chapter is about how we slowly discovered what that story truly meant.

2017 was one of the most important years of our lives.

It was during this time that I began to notice something changing between us. Little by little, our friendship started becoming something deeper. The conversations felt different. The connection felt different. And although neither of us fully understood where the future would take us, something beautiful was quietly growing.

The truth is that I liked you very much.

More than I was willing to admit, even to myself.

But along with those feelings came fear.

You were younger, and I often wondered whether these emotions were simply part of growing up. I worried about making the wrong decision. I worried about your future. Most of all, I worried about being unfair to someone I cared about so deeply.

So I remained silent.

Perhaps one of the hardest things I have ever done was pretending not to say the words that my heart already knew.

That same year, you were preparing for your JSC examinations. I watched you work hard, stay determined, and push yourself forward. Alhamdulillah, you performed well, and I was incredibly proud of you.

But when I think about 2017, it is not the examination that I remember most.

It is the day I came to teach you for what we believed would be the last time.

I still remember the sadness of that moment. Neither of us knew whether our paths would cross again. We did not know if this chapter of our lives was ending forever.

Yet Alhamdulillah, ALLAH had written something different for us.

At the beginning of 2018, I was once again given the opportunity to teach you. What felt impossible only a few months earlier suddenly became reality, and our journey continued.

If 2017 was important, then 2018 was truly special.

It was the year when we finally began to understand what we meant to each other.

For the first time, our feelings were no longer hidden behind friendship alone. We both knew there was something deeper connecting us.

Even then, I was still the same reserved person. I cared for you deeply, but I rarely said the words directly. Looking back now, I think you understood my feelings long before I was brave enough to express them.

Then, toward the end of 2018, you moved to Qatar.

I cannot fully describe how empty that felt.

It was during that separation that I finally understood the depth of my feelings. When someone becomes part of your everyday life, you do not always realize how much they mean to you until they are suddenly far away.

For the first time, I truly felt your absence.

The days felt quieter.

The conversations felt shorter.

And I realized that a part of my happiness had quietly become connected to yours.

As 2018 came to an end, I carried that feeling with me.

Then came 2019, and Alhamdulillah, you returned.

Your mother and brother arrived later, but you came back first. And with your return, a part of my world felt complete again.

Our journey continued, and I watched you grow—not only as a student, but as a person.

Year after year, I saw your dedication reflected in your studies. I saw your determination, your kindness, and your ability to overcome challenges. Every achievement made me proud, and every step forward reminded me how fortunate I was to witness your journey.

Then came 2020.

It was another important milestone in your life.

You sat for your SSC examinations, and Alhamdulillah, you achieved an A+ result. I was incredibly proud of you. You proved what I had always known—that when you set your mind to something, you are capable of achieving remarkable things.

Later that year, you began college.

At the same time, the world was changing because of the pandemic. Life became uncertain, routines disappeared, and suddenly everything felt different from what we had known before.

Yet somehow, our connection remained.

For the first time, you had your own phone.

You would send me messages almost every day.

And if I am being honest, I was still afraid.

Afraid of my feelings.

Afraid of what the future might hold.

Afraid of how much you had already become a part of my life.

So sometimes I took longer than I should have to reply.

But despite all of that, something beautiful was happening.

The distance between us was shrinking.

Not physically, but emotionally.

Conversation by conversation.

Message by message.

Day by day.

Without realizing it, we were becoming closer than ever before.

And little did we know that the years ahead would test us, strengthen us, challenge us, and ultimately bring us even closer together.
